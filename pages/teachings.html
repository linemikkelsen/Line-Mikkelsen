Teachings
My teaching reflects and complements my research, broader language interests and language activities.

I teach syntax at all levels, from a large undergraduate course to graduate seminars. In all of these settings, there is a focus on the interactions between lesser-studied languages and syntactic theory.

I co-teach an interdisciplinary graduate-level course on Indigenous Language Revitalization with colleagues from the Linguistics and English departments and from the Graduate School of Education. This is the core course for the Designated Emphasis in Indigenous Language Revitalization.

Based on my work with individual language communities, I teach an upper-division linguistics course on the structure, history and sociolinguistics of a particular language. I have taught this course on Danish, Kalaallisut and Karuk.

Finally, I teach "The American Languages" course, a lower-division course that introduces students of all backgrounds and stages of study to the richness and complexity of language in the US. The course satisfied the American Cultures Requirement.

Most of these classes include participation by members of the language communities I work with and student engagement with primary linguistic material.
